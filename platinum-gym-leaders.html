Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Water, Grass, or Fighting types. Piplup or Turtwig make this easy. With Chimchar, use Mach Punch. Danger: Cranidos has very high Attack — Headbutt can KO fragile Pokémon. Don't underestimate it despite its low level.

PokémonLevelType

Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Fire, Flying, or Ice types. Staravia or Ponyta (Route 206) work great. Danger: Roserade is surprisingly powerful with high Sp.Atk. It knows Grass Knot which does more damage to heavy Pokémon. Stun Spore can cripple your team.

#3

Fantina

Hearthome City
GhostLevel Cap: 26
PokémonLevelType
DuskullLv. 24Ghost
HaunterLv. 24Ghost Poison
MismagiusLv. 26Ghost
Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Dark types. Dark options are limited this early — train a Shinx to learn Bite, or use Turtwig line (also learns Bite). Danger: Mismagius knows Shadow Ball and Psybeam. Don't use Normal or Fighting moves — they do nothing to Ghosts. A Pokémon with Pursuit can trap them.

#4

Veilstone City
FightingLevel Cap: 32
PokémonLevelType
MedititeLv. 28Fighting Psychic
MachokeLv. 29Fighting
LucarioLv. 32Fighting Steel
Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Flying or Psychic types. Staravia/Staraptor is the best option. Danger: Lucario is Fighting/Steel with high Attack and Sp.Atk. It knows Drain Punch and Metal Claw. Ground and Fire are super effective against Lucario specifically. Meditite has Pure Power which doubles its Attack.

#5

Crasher Wake

Pastoria City
WaterLevel Cap: 37
PokémonLevelType
GyaradosLv. 33Water Flying
QuagsireLv. 34Water Ground
FloatzelLv. 37Water
Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Electric or Grass types. Gyarados is 4× weak to Electric. Quagsire is 4× weak to Grass but immune to Electric! Danger: You NEED both Electric AND Grass coverage. Electric alone won't work because of Quagsire. Floatzel is fast with Swift Swim in rain.

#6

SteelLevel Cap: 41
PokémonLevelType
MagnetonLv. 37Electric Steel
SteelixLv. 38Steel Ground

Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Fire, Fighting, or Ground types. No single type beats all three. Magneton takes Ground 4×. Steelix takes Water and Fire. Bastiodon takes Fighting and Ground 4×. Danger: Bastiodon is extremely bulky. Gastrodon with Earth Power is the best all-around counter for this gym.

PokémonLevelType

PiloswineLv. 40Ice Ground

Nuzlocke Strategy

Use Fire or Fighting types. Infernape is the perfect counter. Abomasnow is 4× weak to Fire. Danger: Abomasnow sets up Hail with Snow Warning, damaging your team every turn. Froslass is fast and knows Shadow Ball — don't use Psychic types against it. Sneasel is 4× weak to Fighting.

Use Ground types — complete immunity to Electric. Gastrodon, Hippowdon, or Garchomp are ideal. Danger: Electivire knows Ice Punch which hits Ground types super effectively! Bring a Ground type that can survive an Ice Punch. Jolteon is extremely fast.

Champion — Cynthia
★

Cynthia

Champion — Mixed Types
PokémonLevelType
SpiritombLv. 58Ghost Dark
RoseradeLv. 58Grass Poison
TogekissLv. 60Normal Flying
LucarioLv. 60Fighting Steel
MiloticLv. 58Water
GarchompLv. 62Dragon Ground
Strategy

THE HARDEST CHAMPION IN ALL POKÉMON GAMES. Cynthia's team has almost no shared weaknesses. Spiritomb in Gen 4 has NO weaknesses (Fairy didn't exist). Garchomp at Lv.62 is the most feared Pokémon in Platinum — 4× weak to Ice, but it's so fast it may attack first. Bring Ice Beam on your fastest Pokémon. Togekiss with Serene Grace has 60% flinch with Air Slash. Milotic is extremely bulky with Recover. Key: You MUST have Ice coverage for Garchomp, Electric for Milotic, and Fire/Ground for Lucario. Expect to lose Pokémon in this fight even with preparation.
