Nuzlocke Rules for Infinite Fusion

The fusion mechanic requires special Nuzlocke rules. Here are the most commonly used rule sets:

Standard Fusion Nuzlocke Rules

Catch Rule: You can only catch the first non-fused Pokemon encountered in each area. If a wild fusion appears, skip it and wait for a regular Pokemon.

Fusion Rule: You may fuse any two Pokemon you own using DNA Splicers. If a fused Pokemon faints, BOTH component Pokemon are considered dead.

Unfusion Rule: You may unfuse Pokemon freely, but both components retain any damage taken while fused.

Death Rule: Standard Nuzlocke — if it faints, it is dead. Box it permanently.

Advanced Fusion Rules (Optional)

Fixed Fusion: Once fused, Pokemon cannot be separated. This adds more weight to fusion decisions.

Fusion Only: You can only use fused Pokemon in battle. Unfused Pokemon must stay in the box.

Best Early Encounters for Fusion Nuzlockes

Kanto (First Half)

Route 1 - Nidoran: Both male and female versions are excellent fusion bases. Nidoking/Nidoqueen fusions gain amazing type coverage.

Viridian Forest - Pikachu: Electric typing fuses well with almost anything. Pikachu fusions tend to be fast and hit hard.

Mt. Moon - Clefairy: Fairy type (added in Infinite Fusion) makes Clefairy fusions surprisingly tanky with great defensive typing.

Route 6 - Honedge: Steel/Ghost is one of the best defensive typings in the game. Honedge fusions are incredibly strong.

Kanto (Second Half)

Safari Zone: Tons of rare encounters. Plan carefully since this is one encounter area. Kangaskhan and Scyther fusions are top tier.

Power Plant - Magnemite: Steel/Electric fusions have incredible resistances. Magnezone fusions are among the best in the game.

Pokemon Mansion - Ditto: While Ditto itself is not great, it can be useful for breeding strategies if your rules allow it.

Johto

Route 29-31: Fresh encounters in Johto let you rebuild your team. Larvitar, Mareep, and Heracross are all excellent fusion bases.

Lake of Rage - Gyarados: The Red Gyarados encounter is fixed. Gyarados fusions with Dragon Dance are devastating.

FAQ

What is Pokemon Infinite Fusion?
Pokemon Infinite Fusion is a fan-made game that lets you fuse any two Pokemon together, creating over 176,000 unique combinations with custom sprites. It features both the Kanto and Johto regions with 16 gym badges to collect.
How do fusion rules work in a Nuzlocke?
The most common rule is: if a fused Pokemon faints, both component Pokemon are considered dead. You can only catch unfused Pokemon as your route encounter. Fusions are allowed freely with DNA Splicers you find or buy.
How many encounter locations does Infinite Fusion have?
Infinite Fusion has 80+ encounter locations spanning both Kanto and Johto regions, including secret areas like the Hidden Forest and Viridian River.
What are the level caps for Infinite Fusion Nuzlocke?
Kanto caps: Pewter 12, Cerulean 22, Vermilion 26, Celadon 35, Fuchsia 38, Saffron 45, Cinnabar 51, Viridian 54, E4 58. Johto continues from 62 to 68.
